#001 In Progress

Context Legibility Dashboard

A tool for making AI system assumptions, limits, and reasoning visible in public-facing systems. Designed for government benefits eligibility chatbots and similar high-stakes contexts.

What it demonstrates

Principle 3: Context Legibility. Shows what "nutrition labels for AI" could look like in practice—not for everyday users, but for oversight, advocacy, and critical moments.

The scenario

A government benefits application chatbot. Citizens need to understand what data the system has, what it assumes about their situation, and how it reached eligibility decisions.

Key features

  • What information the system is using (and where it came from)
  • What assumptions it's making about the user's situation
  • What the system can and cannot do
  • Step-by-step reasoning for decisions
  • Controls for users to correct wrong assumptions

Status

Building React prototype with realistic mock data. Expected completion: November 2025.

#002 Planned

Escalation Pattern Library

A collection of design patterns for when AI systems should refuse tasks, escalate to humans, or admit uncertainty—with working examples for different contexts.

What it demonstrates

Principle 4: Escalation & Refusal Design. How to build systems that know their limits and can gracefully admit when they can't help.

Planned patterns

  • The "I Don't Know" Button: Explicit uncertainty acknowledgment
  • Confidence-based escalation: Automatic handoff at low confidence thresholds
  • Capability boundaries: Clear statements of what system cannot do
  • Graceful refusal: Explaining why and suggesting alternatives

Status

Research and planning phase. Expected start: December 2025.

#003 Planned

Disintermediation Case Studies

Documentation and analysis of where AI mediation creates problems in real government and public service deployments—with design recommendations.

What it demonstrates

Principle 2: Disintermediation Analysis. How to identify where AI mediation separates people from capabilities and relationships—and what to do about it.

Planned case studies

  • Customer service chatbots and loss of human judgment
  • Benefits eligibility systems and due process
  • HR automation and the removal of relationship-building
  • Healthcare triage bots and clinical decision-making

Status

Research phase. Expected start: January 2026.
